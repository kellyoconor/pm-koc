## Question 1: How Do I Demo This to Executives?

### The Framing Problem

This is a pet project — 152 skills stitched together from 6 open-source repos with subagents, automations, and slash commands. The trap is showing the parts list. Execs don't care about 152 skills. They care about what it means.

### The One-Liner

> "Product management is moving from artisanal craft to composable, repeatable, AI-augmented workflows — and here's what that looks like in practice."

### Three Demo Paths (A 3-Act Narrative)

Each demo answers the natural objection raised by the previous one.

#### Act 1: Speed (The Before/After)

**What to show:** Pick ONE workflow. A feature request comes in from sales.

- Old way: Meetings, docs, back-and-forth, 4-8 hours for a first-draft PRD
- New way: `/discover` -> `/write-prd` -> `/pre-mortem` in sequence — minutes, not hours

**What it proves:** AI automates 60-80% of first-draft documentation work. PMs save 6-9 hours/week.

**Natural objection it raises:** "Fast, but is it any good?"

#### Act 2: Depth (The Multi-Perspective Stress Test)

**What to show:** 7 subagents evaluate the same PRD simultaneously.

| Subagent | Question It Asks |
|----------|-----------------|
| customer-advocate | "Does this solve a real customer problem? What's the evidence?" |
| business-strategist | "How does this compete? What's the moat?" |
| data-analyst | "What metrics validate this? How do we measure?" |
| growth-pm | "How do we acquire and activate? What are the loops?" |
| engineering-lead | "Is this buildable? What's the complexity?" |
| design-lead | "Is this intuitive? Accessible?" |
| risk-assessor | "What could fail? What's the pre-mortem?" |

**What it proves:** No PM can hold 7 perspectives simultaneously. This system can. It's not just fast — it's more rigorous than the manual process.

**Natural objection it raises:** "Impressive, but is this a one-off trick?"

#### Act 3: Compounding (The Learning Loop)

**What to show:** How the system improves over time.

- Sprint retros feed lessons into the next sprint's discovery
- Weekly feedback analysis surfaces patterns automatically
- Monthly competitive scans keep strategy current
- Quarterly strategy reviews validate or pivot direction

**What it proves:** This isn't a tool — it's organizational IP that compounds. Every retro, every interview summary, every A/B test analysis makes the next one better.

**The punchline for execs:** Only 3% of product teams operate at the agentic level (Level 5). This system is already there. The question isn't whether AI changes PM — it's whether we're ahead or behind.

## Question 2: What Is the State of the Art on AI Product Requirements?

### The Short Answer

Three forces are converging:

1. **AI automates 60-80% of first-draft documentation.** PRDs, user stories, acceptance criteria — generated by AI, reviewed (not written) by PMs.
2. **The shift from copilot to agentic AI.** The next wave isn't AI that responds to prompts — it's AI that autonomously detects problems, drafts hypotheses, and runs experiments. Only ~25% of tools have meaningful agentic capability today.
3. **The PM role is elevating, not disappearing.** HBR (Feb 2026): PM skills are the key to enterprise AI adoption. The PM who uses AI will replace the PM who doesn't.

### The PRD Itself Has Changed

Modern PRDs (per Miqdad Jaffer/OpenAI, Aakash Gupta):

1. **Lighter and sharper** — Not 10-page specs. Focused on decisions, trade-offs, boundaries.
2. **Example-driven** — For AI features, include example inputs/outputs (AI is probabilistic).
3. **Dual metrics** — Traditional KPIs + AI-quality metrics (accuracy, hallucination rate, latency).
4. **Deployment-aware** — How you launch is part of the requirements.
5. **Living documents** — Continuously updated as context changes.
6. **Machine-readable** — Structured enough for AI agents to parse and act on.

### The Process Has Changed

Best-in-class teams in 2026:

1. **Discovery:** AI continuously ingests customer signals (BuildBetter, Zeda.io). PM directs and interprets.
2. **Requirements:** PM provides a brief. AI generates first-draft PRD in minutes. PM reviews and sharpens.
3. **Decomposition:** AI breaks PRD into epics/stories/acceptance criteria, pushes to Linear/Jira.
4. **Validation:** AI suggests experiments, drafts interview guides.
5. **Iteration:** AI monitors for scope creep, flags edge cases, regenerates docs as requirements evolve.
